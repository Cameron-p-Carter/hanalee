About

Biography

A Life in Music

Music has been at the heart of my life for as long as I can remember. There's something extraordinary about the way a violin can speak — it can tell a story, move a room, or make a quiet moment feel sacred. That connection is what drives everything I do, whether I'm on stage or in the teaching studio.


Some of my most cherished memories have come from performing — touring New Zealand as a soloist with the Bluescope Youth Orchestra, leading the KUSCI Piano Quartet, and playing Mozart's Violin Concerto No. 5 at the Wollongong Town Hall and Bowral Arts Centre. Each experience has shaped who I am as a musician and reminded me why I fell in love with the violin in the first place.

Education

Training & Credentials

I hold an Associate in Music, Australia (AMusA) Diploma with Distinction, awarded in 2022. I'm currently pursuing my Bachelor of Music (Performance) at the Sydney Conservatorium of Music, studying under Professor Goetz Richter, and I was selected for the Ted and Susan Meller Memorial Scholarship Fund (2024–2027).


I continue to refine my craft through masterclasses, collaborative projects, and performance opportunities, always seeking to deepen my artistry and share my love of music with others.
